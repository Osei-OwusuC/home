Introduction

Soccer, also known as football, is the arguably the best sport in the world. Soccer can be played almost anywhere and almost on any terrain. In soccer two teams of 11 players play for 90 minutes trying to score in each other’s neat. And Soccer is one of the most physical sport, you need stamina, endurance and agility to be good at soccer. About 265 people in world play soccer professionally, that is around 4% of the population. More than 207 countries are a part of FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association), which is more than members than the UN.

History

People have been playing versions of soccer for thousands of years and it has evolved many times. No one knows exactly where soccer originated, but historians found that a version of soccer was played in ancient times. Then soccer continued into the Medieval Times, where it started to become popular. And now we have modern soccer with established rules, but soccer still continues to evolve.

Ancient Soccer

Soccer is a simple sport and the basics of soccer have been on Earth for many years. Early versions soccer was first played in ancient Rome and China. Soccer in ancient china is the most similar version to modern soccer. China had a game called cuju which means “kick the ball with foot”, two teams of two to ten try to score on the other team net. In cuju players were not allowed to use their hands. This game was like a training method for soldiers in the military. Medieval Times Soccer

Soccer continued to be played in the Medieval Times. English people are credited to keeping the sport going through the Medieval Times. The sport became really popular in the mid-14th century. Soccer in these days were more violent, anything was allowed as long as it doesn’t led to manslaughter. That means there was more biting, punching and hitting. Soccer games in this era tend to end in riots, so it was banned. The ban did not stop people from playing soccer and at the end of the century it was embedded into their culture.

Modern Soccer

The soccer that is played now is called Modern soccer. In the early 1860s British clubs started making rules, but every club had different rules. To resolve this problem, twelve clubs came together to establish rules, forming the Football Association. After they created the rules, Scotland, Ireland, Wales and England created a group called IFAB (International Football Association Board) that reviews and changes the rules each year. From there soccer started being played in other European countries, like Spain and France, which lead to the creation of FIFA (fédération internationale de football association) in 1904. In 1913, IFAB joined FIFA.

Rules and Objective

The main objective of soccer has not changed too much over the year. The objective is to shoot on the opponent’s net without using your hands. Soccer is now played with two teams of eleven. Like everything throughout time, the rules of soccer has changed. There is now 17 main rules to soccer:

  1. The field of play
  2. The ball
  3. The players
  4. The player’s Equipment
  5. The referee
  6. Other match officials
  7. The duration of the match
  8. The start and restart of play
  9. The ball in and out of play
  10. Determining the outcome of a match
  11. Offside
  12. Fouls and Misconduct
  13. Free kicks
  14. The penalty kick
  15. The throw-in
  16. The Goal kick
  17. The Corner kick

These rules go into detail and give specifications about the field, the ball, the players and their equipment, match officials, how the game will go and misconduct and the punishment.

Formations and Positions

Like many other sports there is positions and formations for soccer. In soccer there is a goalkeeper who is allowed to use his hand to protect the net. Then there is a group of defenders who defend the net. After that there is midfielders who attack and defend. Then there is forwards to attack the other net. These are examples of formations :

This is a basic and balanced formation. It is called 4-4-2, so there is four defenders (backs), four midfielders and 2 forwards (strikers). In the defensive line, there is two center backs (fullback), a left back and a right back. In midfield, there is two center midfielders, a left midfielder and a right midfielder. And in the forward line there is two strikers.

This is an offensive formation. It is called 4-3-3, so there is four defenders, three midfielders, and three forwards. In the defensive line there is two center backs, a left back and a right back. In midfield, there is three center midfielders. And forward, there is one striker and two wingers. Wingers are players who play wider. They tend to be faster and cross to the striker. They are sometimes considered as midfielders instead of forwards.

This formation is a defensive formation. It is called 5-3-2, so there is five defenders, 3 midfielders and 2 forwards. In the defensive line there is three center backs, and two wingbacks. A wingback is a back that plays more wide and forward. They sometimes play alongside midfielders.

Leagues

Currently there is more than 20 professional leagues in the world. The top leagues being English Premier League, BBVA La Liga and Bundesliga . In leagues, soccer clubs play each other and the team with the most points at the end of the season wins. When a team wins they get three points, when they tie, they get one point and when they lose they get zero points. In major leagues, the bottom one to three teams gets regulated to a lower league and some teams from the smaller league gets promoted. English Premier League is the top tier league in England. It is the biggest and one of the most competitive leagues, with 20 teams and generating 2.2 billion euros per year. La Liga is the top tier team in Spain. La Liga teams are often doing the best in club tournaments. It is arguably as competitive as EPL. Two of the best players are found here (Messi and Ronaldo). Bundesliga is the top tier team in Germany. Bundesliga consists of 18 teams, FC Bayern Munich being the top team (25 wins).

Tournaments

Soccer is the most watched sport and tournaments attracts the most viewers. Soccer tournaments are often intense and thrilling. There is club tournaments and league tournaments.

World Cup

The world cup is the most watched tournament in the world. The world cup is an international tournament that takes place every four years. It is organised by FIFA. The last world cup was in 2014. About 204 countries try to qualify for the cup and only 32 make it. About 3.2 billion people watched the world cup in 2014. The latest winner is Germany.

Champions League

Champions League is the second most watched soccer tournament in the world. It is a tournament for the best 32 clubs around the world. It is organised by UEFA. The latest winner is Real Madrid, who have won the most (11 times).
